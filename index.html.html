Recognition
"Nothing fundamental changed. No news broke. But price fell 6% in ninety minutes. Why?"

Most participants look for a story after the move. They find one. It is almost always wrong, or at best, late. The move already happened because of mechanics: a margin call triggered forced selling, a hedging flow cascaded through correlated positions, or a dealer rebalancing shifted the book.

These are not mysterious events. They are structural. They happen on schedules, at thresholds, and in patterns that are readable if you know what layer you are looking at. The narrative arrives after the positioning change. We read the positioning change itself.

The Method

The TexasWest Capital Stack

Four layers. Each one narrows the decision space. Together, they replace speculation with classification.

01

Structure

The regime. Bull, bear, transitional, or range-bound. Structure defines the set of outcomes that are mechanically possible. Everything that follows is bounded by this classification.

02

Mechanics

The plumbing. Liquidity conditions, leverage accumulation, dealer positioning, derivatives exposure, and threshold events where positioning must change. Degrossing does not begin with emotion. It begins when exposure crosses a structural boundary.

03

Social Mood

Not sentiment. Not polls. Social mood is the background emotional state of the market that modulates the speed, violence, and forgiveness of mechanical moves. It does not cause moves. It shapes their character.

04

Risk Posture

The decision layer. Given what structure allows and mechanics require, what is the correct posture for capital? Full exposure, reduced size, hedged, or flat. The most important decision is often to do nothing.

Structure defines what can happen. Social mood determines how violently it happens. Risk posture determines whether capital survives.

Threshold Theory

Markets do not unwind randomly. Exposure builds toward measurable inflection levels. When those thresholds are crossed, positioning changes mechanically. Understanding where pressure accumulates is often more important than predicting direction.

Risk Governance

Every framework inside TexasWest Capital includes degradation protocols and capital preservation rules. If conditions shift, exposure adjusts. If structure breaks, positioning reduces. Survival is engineered, not hoped for.
